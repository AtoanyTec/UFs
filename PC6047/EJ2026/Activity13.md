# 🧠📄 Activity: Identifying Research Methodology Concepts from a Scientific Paper

## 🎯 Objective
Students will analyze a scientific paper to **identify and understand the core components of research methodology**, connecting theory (what they’ve learned) with real-world research.

---

## 🧩 Activity Description

Students will be provided with a **scientific paper**. Based on this paper, they must identify and explain the key elements of research methodology, such as:

- Research problem
- Research question
- Objectives
- Hypothesis (if applicable)
- Variables (independent/dependent)
- Methodology
- Research approach (qualitative, quantitative, mixed)
- Scope (exploratory, descriptive, correlational, explanatory)
- Literature review
- Ethical considerations

---

## 🪜 Instructions (Step-by-Step)

1. **Read the assigned paper carefully** (individual work).
2. Identify the following elements within the paper:
   - What problem is being addressed?
   - What is the research question?
   - What are the objectives?
   - Is there a hypothesis?
   - What variables are involved?
   - What methodology is used?
   - What type of research approach and scope is applied?
   - What prior work (literature review) is referenced?
   - Are there any ethical considerations mentioned?
3. Extract **evidence from the paper** (quotes or paraphrased sections).
4. Reflect on:
   - Is the methodology appropriate for the problem?
   - What could be improved?

---

## 📦 Deliverables

### 1. 📝 Analysis Report (Individual)
**Format:** PDF (1–2 pages)

Include:
- Title and student name
- Table or structured sections with:
  - Each research component
  - Identified content from the paper
  - Brief explanation in your own words
- Short critical reflection (5–7 lines)

---

### 2. 📊 Visual Summary (Optional but recommended)
Choose one:
- Concept map
- Diagram
- Infographic

**Goal:** Show how all research elements are connected

---

### 3. 🎤 Mini Presentation (In class or recorded)
**Duration:** 3–5 minutes

Explain:
- Key findings
- Most interesting aspect of the research
- One critique or improvement
